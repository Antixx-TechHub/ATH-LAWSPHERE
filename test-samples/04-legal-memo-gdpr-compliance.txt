EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
================================================================================

This memorandum outlines the legal requirements under the General Data Protection 
Regulation (GDPR) that DataTech Solutions must comply with when processing 
personal data of European Union residents. Given our expansion into European 
markets and our SaaS platform serving EU customers, GDPR compliance is mandatory 
and failure to comply can result in penalties up to €20 million or 4% of global 
annual turnover, whichever is higher.

================================================================================
BACKGROUND
================================================================================

DataTech Solutions India Pvt. Ltd. ("Company") is a software-as-a-service (SaaS) 
provider offering cloud-based CRM and analytics solutions. The Company currently:

1. Serves 150+ enterprise customers in the EU
2. Processes personal data of approximately 500,000 EU data subjects
3. Stores data in AWS Mumbai (ap-south-1) and AWS Ireland (eu-west-1) regions
4. Employs 50+ personnel who access EU customer data
5. Uses third-party sub-processors for email, analytics, and payment processing

================================================================================
KEY GDPR REQUIREMENTS APPLICABLE TO THE COMPANY
================================================================================

ARTICLE 5 - PRINCIPLES OF DATA PROCESSING:

1. Lawfulness, Fairness, and Transparency
   - Processing must have a legal basis (Article 6)
   - Privacy notices must clearly explain data processing
   - ACTION: Review and update all customer-facing privacy policies

2. Purpose Limitation
   - Data collected for specific purposes only
   - Cannot be processed for incompatible purposes
   - ACTION: Audit current data uses against stated purposes

3. Data Minimization
   - Collect only necessary data
   - ACTION: Review data collection forms and eliminate unnecessary fields

4. Accuracy
   - Keep data accurate and up-to-date
   - ACTION: Implement data quality procedures

5. Storage Limitation
   - Retain only as long as necessary
   - ACTION: Implement data retention and deletion policies

6. Integrity and Confidentiality (Security)
   - Appropriate technical and organizational measures
   - ACTION: Security audit and enhancement

================================================================================
LEGAL BASIS FOR PROCESSING (ARTICLE 6)
================================================================================

The Company relies on the following legal bases:

1. CONTRACT PERFORMANCE (Article 6(1)(b))
   - Processing necessary for SaaS service delivery
   - Customer account management
   - Billing and invoicing

2. LEGITIMATE INTERESTS (Article 6(1)(f))
   - Product improvement and analytics
   - Fraud prevention and security
   - REQUIREMENT: Document Legitimate Interest Assessment (LIA)

3. CONSENT (Article 6(1)(a))
   - Marketing communications
   - Non-essential cookies
   - REQUIREMENT: Implement robust consent management

================================================================================
INTERNATIONAL DATA TRANSFERS (CHAPTER V)
================================================================================

ISSUE: Data transfers from EU (Ireland) to India (Mumbai) require legal mechanism.

RECOMMENDED APPROACH:

1. STANDARD CONTRACTUAL CLAUSES (SCCs)
   - Adopt new 2021 EU SCCs (Commission Implementing Decision 2021/914)
   - Module 2: Controller to Processor (for customer data)
   - Module 3: Processor to Processor (for sub-processor relationships)
   
2. SUPPLEMENTARY MEASURES (per Schrems II)
   - Encryption of data in transit and at rest
   - Pseudonymization where possible
   - Access controls limiting India-based access
   - Transfer Impact Assessment (TIA) required

3. DOCUMENTATION REQUIRED:
   - Updated Data Processing Agreements with EU customers
   - Sub-processor agreements with SCCs
   - Transfer Impact Assessment report
   - Record of Processing Activities (Article 30)

================================================================================
DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS (ARTICLES 12-23)
================================================================================

RIGHTS TO BE IMPLEMENTED:

1. Right of Access (Article 15)
   - Respond within 1 month
   - Provide copy of personal data
   - CURRENT GAP: No automated self-service portal

2. Right to Rectification (Article 16)
   - Correct inaccurate data promptly
   - CURRENT STATUS: Partially implemented in CRM settings

3. Right to Erasure/"Right to be Forgotten" (Article 17)
   - Delete data upon valid request
   - CURRENT GAP: No automated deletion workflow
   - COMPLEXITY: Backup systems and audit logs

4. Right to Restriction (Article 18)
   - Mark data for restricted processing
   - CURRENT GAP: Not implemented

5. Right to Data Portability (Article 20)
   - Provide data in machine-readable format
   - CURRENT STATUS: Manual export available

6. Right to Object (Article 21)
   - Stop processing for legitimate interests
   - Mandatory for direct marketing
   - CURRENT GAP: No opt-out mechanism for analytics

================================================================================
DATA PROTECTION OFFICER (ARTICLE 37)
================================================================================

REQUIREMENT ANALYSIS:

The Company MUST appoint a DPO because:
1. Core activities involve large-scale processing of personal data
2. Regular and systematic monitoring of data subjects

DPO REQUIREMENTS:
- Independent position, reporting to highest management
- Expert knowledge of data protection law
- No conflict of interest
- Can be employee or external service provider
- Must be accessible to supervisory authorities

RECOMMENDATION: Appoint qualified external DPO service provider initially, 
with option to hire internal DPO as operations scale.

================================================================================
DATA PROCESSING AGREEMENTS (ARTICLE 28)
================================================================================

As a DATA PROCESSOR for EU customers, the Company must:

1. Have written DPA with each customer (controller)
2. Include mandatory Article 28(3) clauses:
   - Process only on documented instructions
   - Confidentiality obligations
   - Security measures
   - Sub-processor requirements
   - Assistance with data subject rights
   - Audit rights
   - Data return/deletion upon termination

CURRENT STATUS: Existing DPA needs updating to include:
- New SCC modules
- Supplementary measures for India transfers
- Sub-processor notification procedures

SECURITY MEASURES (ARTICLE 32)
================================================================================

REQUIRED TECHNICAL MEASURES:
□ Encryption at rest (AES-256)
□ Encryption in transit (TLS 1.3)
□ Access controls and authentication (MFA)
□ Regular security testing
□ Backup and recovery procedures
□ Incident detection and response

REQUIRED ORGANIZATIONAL MEASURES:
□ Information security policies
□ Employee training
□ Access management procedures
□ Vendor management
□ Physical security

CURRENT GAPS IDENTIFIED:
1. No formal ISMS (recommend ISO 27001 certification)
2. Incomplete employee training records
3. Vendor security assessments not documented

================================================================================

3. Maintain breach register

REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION:

- Notification templates
